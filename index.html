Body Mass Index (BMI)

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple index of weight-for-height that is commonly used to classify underweight, overweight, and obesity in adults. It is defined as the weight in kilograms divided by the square of the height in meters (kg/m²).

Formula

The formula to calculate BMI is:

\( \text{BMI} = \frac{\text{weight (kg)}}{(\text{height (m)})^2} \)

Classification

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the BMI classification is:

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 – 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or greater

Importance of BMI

BMI is a useful measure of overweight and obesity. It provides a simple numeric measure of a person's thickness or thinness, allowing health professionals to discuss weight problems more objectively with their patients. However, BMI does not measure body fat directly, and it is not diagnostic of the health of an individual.
